At First
I earned my JD at Stanford Law School in 1976 when I was 32. After practicing law for 20 years, in San Francisco, Washington, DC and New York, I took a different path. In 1998, at 54, I graduated from Smith College School for Social Work and took up practice as a psychotherapist. I joyfully maintain a full-time private practice, in Dupont Circle, Washington, DC, specializing in the treatment of trauma-based disorders.

And Then 

One winter day in 2016, I heard myself say 'Maybe I can learn to paint.' It turned out I could. In painting, I have found what appears to be a bottomless well of delight. Words cannot convey how grateful I am for the privilege that has allowed me to live in a time and place which affords openings like I found in painting to those of us, like me, who are 'coming into the end zone.'
by Sheila Cahill

About The Work

Having first picked up a paintbrush when I was 72, I approached each canvas eagerly, but mostly in a ‘blank slate’ state of mind. I would start with some paint. Then some more paint. Within minutes, the paint would be telling me what to do. I often felt less like a painter than a paintee. With the benefit of hindsight, I see that the creative path I am on always has been informed by a sensibility that is uniquely mine. All my life, I have tended toward the long view, understanding that If the 4.6 billion years of our earth’s existence were condensed into 24 hours, humans would have been upright and thinking (more or less) for just 78 seconds
by Sheila Cahill
